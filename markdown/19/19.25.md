The transformations in ¬ß 19.7(ii) result from the symmetry and homogeneity of functions on the right-hand sides of ( 19.25.5 ), ( 19.25.7 ), and ( 19.25.14 ). For example, if we write ( 19.25.5 ) as


<a id="E17"></a>
$$

with


<a id="E18"></a>
$$
(x,y,z)=(c-1,c-k^{2},c), \tag{19.25.18}
$$

then the five nontrivial permutations of $x,y,z$ that leave $R_{F}$ invariant change $k^{2}$ ( $=(z-y)/(z-x)$ ) into $1/k^{2}$ , ${k^{\prime}}^{2}$ , $1/{k^{\prime}}^{2}$ , $-k^{2}/{k^{\prime}}^{2}$ , $-{k^{\prime}}^{2}/k^{2}$ , and $\sin\phi$ ( $=\sqrt{(z-x)/z}$ ) into $k\sin\phi$ , $-i\tan\phi$ , $-ik^{\prime}\tan\phi$ , $(k^{\prime}\sin\phi)/\sqrt{1-k^{2}{\sin}^{2}\phi}$ , $-ik\sin\phi/\sqrt{1-k^{2}{\sin}^{2}\phi}$ . Thus the five permutations induce five transformations of Legendre‚Äôs integrals (and also of the Jacobian elliptic functions).

The three changes of parameter of $\Pi\left(\phi,\alpha^{2},k\right)$ in ¬ß 19.7(iii) are unified in ( 19.21.12 ) by way of ( 19.25.14 ).
