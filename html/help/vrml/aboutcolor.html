Phase Mappings

By painting the surfaces with a color that encodes the phase, ph‚Å°f, both the magnitude and phase of complex valued functions can be displayed. We offer two options for encoding the phase.

Four Color Phase Mapping

The four color scheme quickly indicates in which quadrant z lies: the colors blue, green, red and yellow are used to indicate the first, second, third and fourth quadrants, respectively. As a mnemonic, the colors are sorted alphabetically.

Continuous Phase Mapping

For the continuous phase mapping, we map the phase continuously onto the hue, as both are periodic.
