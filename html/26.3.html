§26.3(i) Definitions

ⓘ
Keywords:
binomial coefficients, relation to lattice paths
Notes:
See Comtet (1974, pp. 8, 22–23). Tables 26.3.1 and 26.3.2 are from Abramowitz and Stegun (1964, Table 24.1).
Referenced by:
§1.2(i)
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/26.3.i
See also:
Annotations for §26.3 and Ch.26

(mn) is the number of ways of choosing n objects from a collection of m distinct objects without regard to order. (m+nn) is the number of lattice paths from (0,0) to (m,n). See also 1.2(i). The number of lattice paths from (0,0) to (m,n), m≤n, that stay on or above the line y=x is (m+nm)−(m+nm−1).
