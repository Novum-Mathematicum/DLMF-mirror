§27.10 Periodic Number-Theoretic Functions

ⓘ
Keywords:
Dirichlet character, Dirichlet characters, Fourier series, Gauss sum, Gauss sums, Ramanujan’s sum, finite, finite Fourier series, number theory, number-theoretic functions, periodic, separable, separable Gauss sum
Notes:
See Apostol (1976, Chapter 8).

If k is a fixed positive integer, then a number-theoretic function f is periodic (mod k) if

27.10.1 f⁡(n+k)=f⁡(n),
n=1,2,….
ⓘ
Symbols:
k: positive integer, n: positive integer and f⁡(n): function
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/27.10.E1

Examples are the Dirichlet characters (mod k) and the greatest common divisor (n,k) regarded as a function of n.

Every function periodic (mod k) can be expressed as a finite Fourier series of the form

27.10.2 f⁡(n)=∑m=1kg⁡(m)⁢e2⁢π⁢i⁢m⁢n/k,
ⓘ
Symbols:
π: the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter, e: base of natural logarithm, i: imaginary unit, k: positive integer, m: positive integer, n: positive integer, f⁡(n): function and g⁡(m): periodic function

where g⁡(m) is also periodic (mod k), and is given by

27.10.3 g⁡(m)=1k⁢∑n=1kf⁡(n)⁢e−2⁢π⁢i⁢m⁢n/k.

An example is Ramanujan’s sum:

27.10.4 ck⁡(n)=∑m=1kχ1⁡(m)⁢e2⁢π⁢i⁢m⁢n/k,

27.10.5 ck⁡(n)=∑d|(n,k)d⁢μ⁡(kd).

Another generalization of Ramanujan’s sum is the Gauss sum G⁡(n,χ) associated with a Dirichlet character χ(modk). It is defined by the relation

27.10.9 G⁡(n,χ)=∑m=1kχ⁡(m)⁢e2⁢π⁢i⁢m⁢n/k.
ⓘ
Defines:
G⁡(n,χ): Gauss sum
Symbols:
χ⁡(n): Dirichlet character, π: the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter, e: base of natural logarithm, i: imaginary unit, k: positive integer, m: positive integer, n: positive integer and χ: Dirichlet character

In particular, G⁡(n,χ1)=ck⁡(n).

G⁡(n,χ) is separable for some n if

27.10.10 G⁡(n,χ)=χ¯⁢(n)⁢G⁡(1,χ).
ⓘ
Symbols:
χ⁡(n): Dirichlet character, G⁡(n,χ): Gauss sum, z¯: complex conjugate, n: positive integer and χ: Dirichlet character
Permalink:

For any Dirichlet character χ(modk), G⁡(n,χ) is separable for n if (n,k)=1, and is separable for every n if and only if G⁡(n,χ)=0 whenever (n,k)>1. For a primitive character χ(modk), G⁡(n,χ) is separable for every n, and

27.10.11 |G⁡(1,χ)|2=k.
ⓘ
Symbols:
χ⁡(n): Dirichlet character, G⁡(n,χ): Gauss sum, k: positive integer and χ: Dirichlet character
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/27.10.E11
Encodings:

Conversely, if G⁡(n,χ) is separable for every n, then χ is primitive (mod k).

The finite Fourier expansion of a primitive Dirichlet character χ(modk) has the form

27.10.12 χ⁡(n)=G⁡(1,χ)k⁢∑m=1kχ¯⁢(m)⁢e−2⁢π⁢i⁢m⁢n/k.
ⓘ
