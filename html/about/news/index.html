2015-07-01 Launch of DLMF Standard Reference Tables web service

Version 1.0 (beta) of the DLMF Standard Reference Tables web service (DLMF Tables) has been released. Its goal is to provide a standard of comparison for testing numerical software by computing, on demand, values of selected special functions to user-defined precision with guaranteed error bounds.

DLMF Tables has the following features:

  1. 1.

    arguments are read from web form or uploaded file;

  2. 2.

    user-specified precision (up to 500 digits);

  3. 3.

    computation errors (truncation and rounding) are analyzed a priori to satisfy the required accuracy;

  4. 4.

    provides strict high-precision enclosures (upper/lower bounds);

  5. 5.

    optional round to nearest or directed roundings;

  6. 6.

    comparison to user-supplied function values, highlighting any differences and providing approximate relative errors;

  7. 7.

    links to function definitions in http://dlmf.nist.gov/, the NIST Digital Library of Mathematical Functions.

DLMF Tables is available at http://dlmftables.uantwerpen.be/

It is a result of collaboration between the NIST Applied and Computational Mathematics Division and the University of Antwerp Computational Mathematics Research Group. Any comments on the new service will be gratefully received at DLMFTables Feedback.

2011-03-21 Firefox 3 users should upgrade

The just-released version 4 of Firefox fixes a disturbing MathML display bug. Previously, unless STIX fonts were installed, the surd for the square root of a fraction would not stretch enough to cover both the numerator and denominator. Users of Firefox before version 4 should upgrade; when that is not possible, installing the STIX fonts is recommended (See Special fonts for MathML).

2008-11-07 Call for proposals to publish a printed version of the DLMF. (Updated 2008-11-21)

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) seeks proposals from publishers interested in licensing the rights to produce and market a printed version of the NIST Digital Library of Mathematical Functions (DLMF). For complete information see Public Announcement.

Clarifications:

For the 3D graphics in the book, do they require 3D glasses?

No. Although an interesting idea, there is no consideration of special viewing aids for the book version of the DLMF.

If all the material in the book will be on the web, do you really want to add the expense of a DVD to the book?

Please understand that the DVD (or other suitable media, such as CD) will contain only PDF files of the book version of the DLMF. There is no intention to reproduce the Web version for distribution with the book. The Web version will be accessible from the NIST DLMF website and, potentially, from NIST-authorized mirror sites.

What is the total number of figures and the total number of color figures? Ballpark figures are fine.

By a recent approximate count, there are 409 figures. Of these, 347 include color. The 3D surfaces with continuous color account for 140 of the color figures; 2D line graphs and diagrams with solid-colored lines account for the remainder.

On page 5 of the Public Announcement, it‚Äôs stated, under Initial Production, that a proposal for a cover design is required and is to be based on the Sample Chapter Collection. The delivery date for the cover design is to be included in the Production Plan. Does this mean a draft design based on the Sample is required as part of the solicitation process?

No. The purpose of the itemized lists on pages 5 and 6 is to describe the essential elements of the license agreement that will be drawn up by NIST and the selected licensee. The proposal for cover design is required after a licensee has been selected as a result of the solicitation process. Only the delivery date of the cover design proposal is required now. This date can be indicated, for example, as ‚Äúxxx days after notification of selection by NIST‚Äù.
