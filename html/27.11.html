The behavior of a number-theoretic function f⁡(n) for large n is often difficult to determine because the function values can fluctuate considerably as n increases. It is more fruitful to study partial sums and seek asymptotic formulas of the form

Each of (27.11.13)–(27.11.15) is equivalent to the prime number theorem (27.2.3). The prime number theorem for arithmetic progressions—an extension of (27.2.3) and first proved in de la Vallée Poussin (1896a, b)—states that if (h,k)=1, then the number of primes p≤x with p≡h(modk) is asymptotic to x/(ϕ⁡(k)⁢ln⁡x) as x→∞.
