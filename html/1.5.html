Implicit Function Theorem

ⓘ
Keywords:
implicit function theorem, neighborhood
See also:
Annotations for §1.5(i), §1.5 and Ch.1

If F⁡(x,y) is continuously differentiable, F⁡(a,b)=0, and ∂F/∂y≠0 at (a,b), then in a neighborhood of (a,b), that is, an open disk centered at a,b, the equation F⁡(x,y)=0 defines a continuously differentiable function y=g⁡(x) such that F⁡(x,g⁡(x))=0, b=g⁡(a), and g′⁡(x)=−Fx/Fy.
