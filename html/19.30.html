The arclength s of the ellipse

19.30.1 x =a⁢sin⁡ϕ,
y =b⁢cos⁡ϕ,
0≤ϕ≤2⁢π,

with a>b, is given by

19.30.2 s=a⁢∫0ϕ1−k2⁢sin2⁡θ⁢dθ.
ⓘ
Symbols:
dx: differential of x, ∫: integral, sin⁡z: sine function, ϕ: real or complex argument, k: real or complex modulus and s: arclength

19.30.3 s/a=E⁡(ϕ,k)=RF⁡(c−1,c−k2,c)−13⁢k2⁢RD⁡(c−1,c−k2,c),

Cancellation on the second right-hand side of (19.30.3) can be avoided by use of (19.25.10).

The length of the ellipse is

19.30.5 L⁡(a,b)=4⁢a⁢E⁡(k)=8⁢a⁢RG⁡(0,b2/a2,1)=8⁢RG⁡(0,a2,b2)=8⁢a⁢b⁢RG⁡(0,a−2,b−2),
ⓘ
Symbols:
RG⁡(x,y,z): symmetric elliptic integral of second kind, E⁡(k): Legendre’s complete elliptic integral of the second kind, k: real or complex modulus and L⁡(a,b): length
Referenced by:
§19.15, §19.24(i), §19.30(i), §19.33(i), §19.9(i)
Permalink:

showing the symmetry in a and b. Approximations and inequalities for L⁡(a,b) are given in §19.9(i).

Let a2 and b2 be replaced respectively by a2+λ and b2+λ, where λ∈(−b2,∞), to produce a family of confocal ellipses. As λ increases, the eccentricity k decreases and the rate of change of arclength for a fixed value of ϕ is given by

19.30.6 ∂s∂(1/k)=a2−b2⁢F⁡(ϕ,k)=a2−b2⁢RF⁡(c−1,c−k2,c),
k2=(a2−b2)/(a2+λ), c=csc2⁡ϕ.
ⓘ

RF⁡(x,y,z): symmetric elliptic integral of first kind, csc⁡z: cosecant function, F⁡(ϕ,k): Legendre’s incomplete elliptic integral of the first kind, ∂f∂x: partial derivative of f with respect to x, ∂x: partial differential of x, ϕ: real or complex argument, k: real or complex modulus and s: arclength
