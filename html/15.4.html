Symbols:
Γ⁡(z): gamma function, F⁡(a,b;c;z) or F⁡(a,bc;z): =F12⁡(a,b;c;z) Gauss’ hypergeometric function, π: the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter, e: base of natural logarithm, i: imaginary unit and a: real or complex parameter
Proof sketch:
From §15.5(ii) one obtains that w⁢(a)=e−i⁢π⁢a/2⁢2−2⁢a⁢3(3⁢a+1)/2⁢F⁡(3⁢a,a;2⁢a;ei⁢π/3)⁢Γ⁡(13+a)/Γ⁡(12+a) is a solution of the difference equation (3⁢a+5)⁢w⁢(a+2)−6⁢(a+1)⁢w⁢(a+1)+(3⁢a+1)⁢w⁢(a)=0. Two linearly independent solutions of this difference equation are w1⁢(a)=1 and w2⁢(a)=Γ⁡(13+a)/Γ⁡(23+a). Combining (15.6.1) with Laplace’s method (see §2.4(iii)) we obtain that w⁢(a)∼π⁢(1Γ⁡(23)+a−1/3Γ⁡(13)), as a→∞. Hence, w⁢(a)=π⁢(1Γ⁡(23)+w2⁢(a)Γ⁡(13)).
Notes:
See §15.2(ii) for treatment of the case when the third parameter is a nonpositive integer.
