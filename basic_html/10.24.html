In consequence of (10.24.6), when x is large J~ν⁡(x) and Y~ν⁡(x) comprise a numerically satisfactory pair of solutions of (10.24.1); compare §2.7(iv). Also, in consequence of (10.24.7)–(10.24.9), when x is small either J~ν⁡(x) and tanh⁡(12⁢π⁢ν)⁢Y~ν⁡(x) or J~ν⁡(x) and Y~ν⁡(x) comprise a numerically satisfactory pair depending whether ν≠0 or ν=0.

For graphs of J~ν⁡(x) and Y~ν⁡(x) see §10.3(iii).

For mathematical properties and applications of J~ν⁡(x) and Y~ν⁡(x), including zeros and uniform asymptotic expansions for large ν, see Dunster (1990a). In this reference J~ν⁡(x) and Y~ν⁡(x) are denoted respectively by Fi⁢ν⁡(x) and Gi⁢ν⁡(x).
