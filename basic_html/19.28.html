§19.28 Integrals of Elliptic Integrals

ⓘ
Keywords:
integrals of, symmetric elliptic integrals
Notes:
To prove (19.28.1)–(19.28.3) from (19.28.4) use §19.16(iii). To prove (19.28.4) expand the R-function in powers of 1−t by (19.19.3), integrate term by term, and use Erdélyi et al. (1953a, 2.8(46)). (19.28.5) is equivalent to (19.18.1). In (19.28.6) let v=u and use Carlson (1963, (7.9)). In (19.28.7) substitute (19.16.2), change the order of integration, and use (19.29.4). Use Carlson (1963, (7.11)) and (19.16.20) to prove (19.28.8) and (19.28.10). In the first case Carlson (1977b, (5.9-21)) is needed; in the second case put (z1,z2,ζ1,ζ2)=(a2,b2,c2,d2), use Carlson (1977b, (9.8-4)), and substitute t=(a⁢b/c⁢d)⁢exp⁡(2⁢z). To prove (19.28.9) from (19.28.10), put a=exp⁡(i⁢x)=1/b, c=exp⁡(i⁢y)=1/d, cosh⁡z=1/sin⁡θ, and on the right-hand side use (19.22.1).
