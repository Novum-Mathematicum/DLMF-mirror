where n is a large positive integer, and the logarithm takes its principal value (§4.2(i)).

Let Pα denote the closure of the domain that is bounded by the parabola y2=4⁢α⁢(x+α) and contains the origin. Then M⁡(a,b,z) has no zeros in the regions Pb/a, if 0<b≤a; P1, if 1≤a≤b; Pα, where α=(2⁢a−b+a⁢b)/(a⁢(a+1)), if 0<a<1 and a≤b<2⁢a/(1−a). The same results apply for the nth partial sums of the Maclaurin series (13.2.2) of M⁡(a,b,z).

More information on the location of real zeros can be found in Zarzo et al. (1995) and Segura (2008).

For the case b≥1 we can use T⁡(a,b)=T⁡(a−b+1,2−b) and P⁡(a,b)=P⁡(a−b+1,2−b).

In Wimp (1965) it is shown that if a,b∈ℝ and 2⁢a−b>−1, then U⁡(a,b,z) has no zeros in the sector |ph⁡z|≤12⁢π.

Inequalities for the zeros of U⁡(a,b,x) are given in Gatteschi (1990). See also Segura (2008).
