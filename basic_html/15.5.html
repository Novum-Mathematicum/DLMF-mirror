By repeated applications of (15.5.11)–(15.5.18) any function F⁡(a+k,b+ℓ;c+m;z), in which k,ℓ,m are integers, can be expressed as a linear combination of F⁡(a,b;c;z) and any one of its contiguous functions, with coefficients that are rational functions of a,b,c, and z.

An equivalent equation to the hypergeometric differential equation (15.10.1) is

15.5.19 z⁢(1−z)⁢(a+1)⁢(b+1)⁢F⁡(a+2,b+2;c+2;z)+(c−(a+b+1)⁢z)⁢(c+1)⁢F⁡(a+1,b+1;c+1;z)−c⁢(c+1)⁢F⁡(a,b;c;z)=0.
ⓘ
