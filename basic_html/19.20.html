where x,y,z may be permuted.

When the variables are real and distinct, the various cases of RJ⁡(x,y,z,p) are called circular (hyperbolic) cases if (p−x)⁢(p−y)⁢(p−z) is positive (negative), because they typically occur in conjunction with inverse circular (hyperbolic) functions. Cases encountered in dynamical problems are usually circular; hyperbolic cases include Cauchy principal values. If x,y,z are permuted so that 0≤x<y<z, then the Cauchy principal value of RJ is given by

19.20.14 (q+z)⁢RJ⁡(x,y,z,−q)=(p−z)⁢RJ⁡(x,y,z,p)−3⁢RF⁡(x,y,z)+3⁢(x⁢y⁢zx⁢y+p⁢q)1/2⁢RC⁡(x⁢y+p⁢q,p⁢q),
