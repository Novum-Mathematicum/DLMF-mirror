§19.7(iii) Change of Parameter of Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k)

ⓘ
Keywords:
Legendre’s elliptic integrals, Legendre’s relation, change of parameter, circular cases, hyperbolic cases
Notes:
All three relations follow from the change of parameter for the symmetric integral of the third kind; see §19.21(iii) and (19.25.14).
Referenced by:
§19.15, §19.25(i), §19.6(iv)
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/19.7.iii
See also:
Annotations for §19.7 and Ch.19

There are three relations connecting Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k) and Π⁡(ϕ,ω2,k), where ω2 is a rational function of α2. If k2 and α2 are real, then both integrals are circular cases or both are hyperbolic cases (see §19.2(ii)).

The first of the three relations maps each circular region onto itself and each hyperbolic region onto the other; in particular, it gives the Cauchy principal value of Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k) when α2>csc2⁡ϕ (see (19.6.5) for the complete case). Let c=csc2⁡ϕ≠α2. Then

19.7.8 Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k)+Π⁡(ϕ,ω2,k)=F⁡(ϕ,k)+c⁢RC⁡((c−1)⁢(c−k2),(c−α2)⁢(c−ω2)),
α2⁢ω2=k2.
ⓘ
Defines:
ω2: change of variable (locally)
Symbols:
RC⁡(x,y): Carlson’s combination of inverse circular and inverse hyperbolic functions, F⁡(ϕ,k): Legendre’s incomplete elliptic integral of the first kind, Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k): Legendre’s incomplete elliptic integral of the third kind, ϕ: real or complex argument, k: real or complex modulus, α2: real or complex parameter and c: change of variable
Referenced by:
§19.25(i), §19.6(i)
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/19.7.E8
Encodings:
TeX, pMML, png
See also:
Annotations for §19.7(iii), §19.7 and Ch.19

Since k2≤c we have α2⁢ω2≤c; hence α2>c implies ω2<1≤c.

The second relation maps each hyperbolic region onto itself and each circular region onto the other:

19.7.9 (k2−α2)⁢Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k)+(k2−ω2)⁢Π⁡(ϕ,ω2,k)=k2⁢F⁡(ϕ,k)−α2⁢ω2⁢c−1⁢RC⁡(c⁢(c−k2),(c−α2)⁢(c−ω2)),
(1−α2)⁢(1−ω2)=1−k2.
ⓘ
Defines:
ω2: change of variable (locally)
Symbols:
RC⁡(x,y): Carlson’s combination of inverse circular and inverse hyperbolic functions, F⁡(ϕ,k): Legendre’s incomplete elliptic integral of the first kind, Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k): Legendre’s incomplete elliptic integral of the third kind, ϕ: real or complex argument, k: real or complex modulus, α2: real or complex parameter and c: change of variable

The third relation (missing from the literature of Legendre’s integrals) maps each circular region onto the other and each hyperbolic region onto the other:

19.7.10 (1−α2)⁢Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k)+(1−ω2)⁢Π⁡(ϕ,ω2,k)=F⁡(ϕ,k)+(1−α2−ω2)⁢c−k2⁢RC⁡(c⁢(c−1),(c−α2)⁢(c−ω2)),
(k2−α2)⁢(k2−ω2)=k2⁢(k2−1).
ⓘ
