The paths of integration are the line segments connecting the limits of integration. The integral for E⁡(ϕ,k) is well defined if k2=sin2⁡ϕ=1, and the Cauchy principal value (§1.4(v)) of Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k) is taken if 1−α2⁢sin2⁡ϕ vanishes at an interior point of the integration path. Also, if k2 and α2 are real, then Π⁡(ϕ,α2,k) is called a circular or hyperbolic case according as α2⁢(α2−k2)⁢(α2−1) is negative or positive. The circular and hyperbolic cases alternate in the four intervals of the real line separated by the points α2=0,k2,1.

The cases with ϕ=π/2 are the complete integrals:

19.2.22 RC⁡(x,y)=2π⁢∫0π/2RC⁡(y,x⁢cos2⁡θ+y⁢sin2⁡θ)⁢dθ.

Symbols:
