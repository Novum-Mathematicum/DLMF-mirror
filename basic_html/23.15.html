The set of all bilinear transformations of this form is denoted by SL(2,ℤ) (Serre (1973, p. 77)).

A modular function f⁡(τ) is a function of τ that is meromorphic in the half-plane ℑ⁡τ>0, and has the property that for all 𝒜∈SL⁢(2,ℤ), or for all 𝒜 belonging to a subgroup of SL(2,ℤ),

23.15.5 f⁡(𝒜τ)=c𝒜⁢(c⁢τ+d)ℓ⁢f⁡(τ),
ℑ⁡τ>0,
ⓘ
Symbols:
ℑ⁡: imaginary part, τ: complex variable, 𝒜: bilinear transformation, c: integer, d: integer, f⁡(τ): modular function, c𝒜: constant and ℓ: level
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/23.15.E5
Encodings:
TeX, pMML, png
See also:
Annotations for §23.15(i), §23.15 and Ch.23

where c𝒜 is a constant depending only on 𝒜, and ℓ (the level) is an integer or half an odd integer. (Some references refer to 2⁢ℓ as the level). If, as a function of q, f⁡(τ) is analytic at q=0, then f⁡(τ) is called a modular form. If, in addition, f⁡(τ)→0 as q→0, then f⁡(τ) is called a cusp form.
