ⓘ
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/32.8.i
See also:
Annotations for §32.8 and Ch.32

PII–PVI possess hierarchies of rational solutions for special values of the parameters which are generated from “seed solutions” using the Bäcklund transformations and often can be expressed in the form of determinants. See Airault (1979).

In the general case assume δ≠0, so that as in §32.2(ii) we may set δ=−12. Then PV has a rational solution iff one of the following holds with m,n∈ℤ and ε=±1:

  1. (a)

    α=12⁢(m+ε⁢γ)2 and β=−12⁢n2, where n>0, m+n is odd, and α≠0 when |m|<n.

  2. (b)

    α=12⁢n2 and β=−12⁢(m+ε⁢γ)2, where n>0, m+n is odd, and β≠0 when |m|<n.

  3. (c)

    α=12⁢a2, β=−12⁢(a+n)2, and γ=m, with m+n even.

  4. (d)

    α=12⁢(b+n)2, β=−12⁢b2, and γ=m, with m+n even.

  5. (e)

    α=18⁢(2⁢m+1)2, β=−18⁢(2⁢n+1)2, and γ∉ℤ.

These rational solutions have the form
