§30.13(i) Prolate Spheroidal Coordinates

ⓘ

Prolate spheroidal coordinates ξ,η,ϕ are related to Cartesian coordinates x,y,z by

30.13.1 x =c⁢(ξ2−1)⁢(1−η2)⁢cos⁡ϕ,
y =c⁢(ξ2−1)⁢(1−η2)⁢sin⁡ϕ,
z =c⁢ξ⁢η,
ⓘ

ξ: prolate spheroidal coordinate (locally), η: prolate spheroidal coordinate (locally) and ϕ: prolate spheroidal coordinate (locally)
Symbols:
cos⁡z: cosine function, sin⁡z: sine function, z: complex variable, x: real variable, y: real variable and c: positive constant

where c is a positive constant. (On the use of the symbol θ in place of ϕ see §1.5(ii).) The (x,y,z)-space without the z-axis corresponds to

30.13.2 1 <ξ<∞,
−1 <η<1,
0 ≤ϕ<2⁢π.
ⓘ
Symbols:
π: the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter, ξ: prolate spheroidal coordinate, η: prolate spheroidal coordinate and ϕ: prolate spheroidal coordinate

The coordinate surfaces ξ=const. are prolate ellipsoids of revolution with foci at x=y=0, z=±c. The coordinate surfaces η=const. are sheets of two-sheeted hyperboloids of revolution with the same foci. The focal line is given by ξ=1, −1≤η≤1, and the rays ±z≥c, x=y=0 are given by η=±1, ξ≥1.
