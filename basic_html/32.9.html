In the case γ=0 and α⁢δ≠0 we assume, as in §32.2(ii), α=1 and δ=−1. Then PIII has algebraic solutions iff

32.9.5 w⁡(z)=Pn2+1⁡(ζ)/Qn2⁡(ζ),
ⓘ

where Pn2+1⁡(ζ) and Qn2⁡(ζ) are polynomials of degrees n2+1 and n2, respectively, with no common zeros. For examples and plots see Clarkson (2003a) and Milne et al. (1997). Similar results hold when δ=0 and β⁢γ≠0.

PIII with β=δ=0 has a first integral

32.9.6 z2⁢(w′)2+2⁢z⁢w⁢w′=(C+2⁢α⁢z⁢w+γ⁢z2⁢w2)⁢w2,
ⓘ
Symbols:
z: real, α: arbitrary constant, γ: arbitrary constant and C: constant
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/32.9.E6

with C an arbitrary constant, which is solvable by quadrature. A similar result holds when α=γ=0. PIII with α=β=γ=δ=0, has the general solution w⁡(z)=C⁢zμ, with C and μ arbitrary constants.
