§10.61(iii) Reflection Formulas for Arguments

ⓘ
Keywords:
Kelvin functions, reflection formulas for arguments and orders
Notes:
See Whitehead (1911).
Permalink:
http://dlmf.nist.gov/10.61.iii
See also:
Annotations for §10.61 and Ch.10

In general, Kelvin functions have a branch point at x=0 and functions with arguments x⁢e±π⁢i are complex. The branch point is absent, however, in the case of berν and beiν when ν is an integer. In particular,

10.61.5 bern⁡(−x) =(−1)n⁢bern⁡x,
bein⁡(−x) =(−1)n⁢bein⁡x.

Symbols:
