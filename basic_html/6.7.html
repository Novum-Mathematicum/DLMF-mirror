Many integrals with exponentials and rational functions, for example, integrals of the type ∫ez⁢R⁡(z)⁢dz, where R⁡(z) is an arbitrary rational function, can be represented in finite form in terms of the function E1⁡(z) and elementary functions; see Lebedev (1965, p. 42).
