§10.42 Zeros

ⓘ
Keywords:
modified Bessel functions, zeros
Notes:
See Watson (1944, pp. 511–513) and Olver (1997b, p. 254).
Referenced by:
Ch.10, Erratum (V1.0.5) for References

Annotations for Ch.10

Properties of the zeros of Iν⁡(z) and Kν⁡(z) may be deduced from those of Jν⁡(z) and Hν(1)⁡(z), respectively, by application of the transformations (10.27.6) and (10.27.8).

For example, if ν is real, then the zeros of Iν⁡(z) are all complex unless −2⁢ℓ<ν<−(2⁢ℓ−1) for some positive integer ℓ, in which event Iν⁡(z) has two real zeros.

The distribution of the zeros of Kn⁡(n⁢z) in the sector −32⁢π≤ph⁡z≤12⁢π in the cases n=1,5,10 is obtained on rotating Figures 10.21.2, 10.21.4, 10.21.6, respectively, through an angle −12⁢π so that in each case the cut lies along the positive imaginary axis. The zeros in the sector −12⁢π≤ph⁡z≤32⁢π are their conjugates.

Kn⁡(z) has no zeros in the sector |ph⁡z|≤12⁢π; this result remains true when n is replaced by any real number ν. For the number of zeros of Kν⁡(z) in the sector |ph⁡z|≤π, when ν is real, see Watson (1944, pp. 511–513).

For z-zeros of Kν⁡(z), with complex ν, see Ferreira and Sesma (2008).

See also Kerimov and Skorokhodov (1984b, a).
