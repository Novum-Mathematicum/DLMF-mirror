In (36.12.10), both second derivatives vanish when critical points coalesce, but their ratio remains finite. The square roots are real and positive when 𝐲 is such that all the critical points are real, and are defined by analytic continuation elsewhere. The quantities am⁡(𝐲) are real for real 𝐲 when g is real analytic.

This technique can be applied to generate a hierarchy of approximations for the diffraction catastrophes ΨK⁡(𝐱;k) in (36.2.10) away from 𝐱=𝟎, in terms of canonical integrals ΨJ⁡(ξ⁡(𝐱;k)) for J<K. For example, the diffraction catastrophe Ψ2⁡(x,y;k) defined by (36.2.10), and corresponding to the Pearcey integral (36.2.14), can be approximated by the Airy function Ψ1⁡(ξ⁡(x,y;k)) when k is large, provided that x and y are not small. For details of this example, see Paris (1991).

For further information see Berry and Howls (1993).
